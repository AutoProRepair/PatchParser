                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>With edismax mm set to 100% if one of the tokens is split into two tokens by the analyzer chain (i.e. "fire-fly" =&gt; fire fly), the mm parameter is ignored and the equivalent of OR query for "fire OR fly" is produced.<br> This is particularly a problem for languages that do not use white space to separate words such as Chinese or Japenese.</p> 
                  <p>See these messages for more discussion:<br> <a href="http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/edismax-parser-ignores-mm-parameter-when-tokenizer-splits-tokens-hypenated-words-WDF-splitting-etc-tc3991911.html" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/edismax-parser-ignores-mm-parameter-when-tokenizer-splits-tokens-hypenated-words-WDF-splitting-etc-tc3991911.html</a></p> 
                  <p><a href="http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/edismax-parser-ignores-mm-parameter-when-tokenizer-splits-tokens-i-e-CJK-tc3991438.html" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/edismax-parser-ignores-mm-parameter-when-tokenizer-splits-tokens-i-e-CJK-tc3991438.html</a></p> 
                  <p><a href="http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/Why-won-t-dismax-create-multiple-DisjunctionMaxQueries-when-autoGeneratePhraseQueries-is-false-tc3992109.html" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">http://lucene.472066.n3.nabble.com/Why-won-t-dismax-create-multiple-DisjunctionMaxQueries-when-autoGeneratePhraseQueries-is-false-tc3992109.html</a></p> 
                 </div>
