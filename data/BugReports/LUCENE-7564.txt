                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>From <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/SOLR-6246" title="Core fails to reload when AnalyzingInfixSuggester is used as a Suggester" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="SOLR-6246"><del>SOLR-6246</del></a>, where AnalyzingInfixSuggester's write lock on its index is causing trouble when reloading a Solr core:</p> 
                  <p><a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/secure/ViewProfile.jspa?name=gsingers" class="user-hover" rel="gsingers">Grant Ingersoll</a> wrote:</p> 
                  <blockquote>
                   <p>One suggestion that might minimize the impact: close the writer after build</p>
                  </blockquote> 
                  <p><a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/secure/ViewProfile.jspa?name=varunthacker" class="user-hover" rel="varunthacker">Varun Thacker</a> wrote:</p> 
                  <blockquote> 
                   <p>This is what I am thinking -</p> 
                   <p>Create a Lucene issue in which <tt>AnalyzingInfixSuggester#build</tt> closes the writer by default at the end.<br> The <tt>add</tt> and <tt>update</tt> methods call <tt>ensureOpen</tt> and those who do frequent real time updates directly via lucene won't see any slowdowns.</p> 
                   <p><a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/secure/ViewProfile.jspa?name=mikemccand" class="user-hover" rel="mikemccand">Michael McCandless</a> - Would this approach have any major drawback from Lucene's perspective? Else I can go ahead an tackle this in a Lucene issue</p>
                  </blockquote> 
                  <p><a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/secure/ViewProfile.jspa?name=mikemccand" class="user-hover" rel="mikemccand">Michael McCandless</a> wrote:</p> 
                  <blockquote>
                   <p>Fixing <tt>AnalyzingInfixSuggester</tt> to close the writer at the end of build seems reasonable?</p>
