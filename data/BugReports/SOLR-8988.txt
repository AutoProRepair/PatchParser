                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>This relates to <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/SOLR-8559" title="FCS facet performance optimization" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="SOLR-8559"><del>SOLR-8559</del></a> – which improves the algorithm used by fcs faceting when <tt>facet.mincount=1</tt></p> 
                  <p>This patch allows <tt>facet.mincount</tt> to be sent as 1 for distributed queries. As far as I can tell there is no reason to set <tt>facet.mincount=0</tt> for refinement purposes . After trying to make sense of all the refinement logic, I cant see how the difference between <em>no value</em> and <em>value=0</em> would have a negative effect.</p> 
                  <p><b>Test perf:</b></p> 
                  <ul class="alternate" type="square"> 
                   <li>~15million unique terms</li> 
                   <li>query matches ~3million documents</li> 
                  </ul> 
                  <p><b>Params:</b></p> 
                  <div class="code panel" style="border-width: 1px;">
                   <div class="codeContent panelContent"> 
                    <pre class="code-java">facet.mincount=1
facet.limit=500
facet.method=fcs
facet.sort=count
</pre> 
                   </div>
                  </div> 
                  <p><b>Average Time Per Request:</b></p> 
                  <ul class="alternate" type="square"> 
                   <li>Before patch: ~20seconds</li> 
                   <li>After patch: &lt;1 second</li> 
                  </ul> 
                  <p><b>Note</b>: all tests pass and in my test, the output was identical before and after patch.</p> 
                 </div> 
                </div>
