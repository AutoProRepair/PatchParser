                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>There have been reports that DERBY html documentation does not render on Internet Explorer 8 and 9.</p> 
                  <p>Kristian reports on IE8<br> Just tried with IE8, and with the Getting Started manual I get this when I view the source:<br> """<br> &lt;!DOCTYPE HTML PUBLIC "-//W3C//DTD HTML 4.0 Transitional//EN"&gt;<br> &lt;HTML&gt;&lt;HEAD&gt;<br> &lt;META content="text/html; charset=utf-8" http-equiv=Content-Type&gt;&lt;/HEAD&gt;<br> &lt;BODY&gt;&lt;/BODY&gt;&lt;/HTML&gt;<br> &lt;snipped garbage as to not put it in jira&gt;</p> 
                  <p>Not sure how that last line will turn out in your email clients, but it is indeed garbage and you can see that the body-tag is empty. Nothing is rendered in the browser. We are using frames though - that's not exactly state-of-the-art... We are also using the wrong DOCTYPE. I'm suspecting that the HTML is invalid too, but haven't checked. </p> 
                  <p>On IE9 I had heard that the reference HTML manual prints garbage.<br> <a href="http://db.apache.org/derby/manuals/index.html#latest" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">http://db.apache.org/derby/manuals/index.html#latest</a></p> 
                 </div> 
                </div>
