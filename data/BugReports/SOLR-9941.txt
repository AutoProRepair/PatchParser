                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>There's kind of an odd situation that arises when a Solr node starts up (after a crash) and tries to recover from it's tlog that causes deletes to be redundantly &amp; excessively applied – at a minimum it causes confusing really log messages....</p> 
                  <ul> 
                   <li><tt>UpdateLog.init(...)</tt> creates <tt>TransactionLog</tt> instances for the most recent log files found (based on numRecordsToKeep) and then builds a <tt>RecentUpdates</tt> instance from them</li> 
                   <li>Delete entries from the <tt>RecentUpdates</tt> are used to populate 2 lists: 
                    <ul> 
                     <li><tt>deleteByQueries</tt></li> 
                     <li><tt>oldDeletes</tt> (for deleteById).</li> 
                    </ul> </li> 
                   <li>Then when <tt>UpdateLog.recoverFromLog</tt> is called a <tt>LogReplayer</tt> is used to replay any (uncommited) <tt>TransactionLog</tt> enteries 
                    <ul> 
                     <li>during replay <tt>UpdateLog</tt> delegates to the UpdateRequestProcessorChain to for the various adds/deletes, etc...</li> 
                     <li>when an add makes it to <tt>RunUpdateProcessor</tt> it delegates to <tt>DirectUpdateHandler2</tt>, which (independent of the fact that we're in log replay) calls <tt>UpdateLog.getDBQNewer</tt> for every add, looking for any "Reordered" deletes that have a version greater then the add 
                      <ul> 
                       <li>if it finds <em>any</em> DBQs "newer" then the document being added, it does a low level <tt>IndexWriter.updateDocument</tt> and then immediately executes <em>all</em> the newer DBQs ... <em>once per add</em></li> 
                      </ul> </li> 
                     <li>these deletes are <b>also</b> still executed as part of the normal tlog replay, because they are in the tlog.</li> 
                    </ul> </li> 
                  </ul> 
                  <p>Which means if you are recovering from a tlog with 90 addDocs, followed by 5 DBQs, then <b>each</b> of those 5 DBQs will each be executed 91 times – and for 90 of those executions, a DUH2 INFO log messages will say <tt>"Reordered DBQs detected. ..."</tt> even tough the only reason they are out of order is because Solr is deliberately applying them out of order.</p> 
                  <ul> 
                   <li>At a minimum we should improve the log messages</li> 
                   <li>Ideally we should stop (pre-emptively) applying these deletes during tlog replay.</li> 
                  </ul> 
                 </div>
