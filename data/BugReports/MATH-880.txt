                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>The 2D polygon difference method is returning incorrect<br> results. Below is a test case of subtracting two polygons (Sorry,<br> this is the simplest case that I could find that duplicates the<br> problem). </p> 
                  <p>There are three problems with the result. The first is that the first<br> point of the first set of vertices is null (and the first point of the<br> second set is also null). The second is that, even if the first null<br> points are ignored, the returned polygon is not the correct result.<br> The first and last points are way off, and the remaining points do not<br> match the original polygon boundaries. Additionally, there are two<br> holes that are returned in the results. This subtraction case should<br> not have holes.</p>
