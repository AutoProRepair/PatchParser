                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>Starting from this commit :</p> 
                  <p><a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/LUCENE-5666" title="Add UninvertingReader" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="LUCENE-5666"><del>LUCENE-5666</del></a>: get solr started<br> git-svn-id: <a href="https://svn.apache.org/repos/asf/lucene/dev/branches/lucene5666@1594254" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">https://svn.apache.org/repos/asf/lucene/dev/branches/lucene5666@1594254</a> 13f79535-47bb-0310-9956-ffa450edef68</p> 
                  <p><a href="https://github.com/apache/lucene-solr/commit/1489085807cb10981a7ea5b5663ada4e3f85953e#diff-5ac9dc7b128b4dd99b764060759222b2" class="external-link" rel="nofollow">https://github.com/apache/lucene-solr/commit/1489085807cb10981a7ea5b5663ada4e3f85953e#diff-5ac9dc7b128b4dd99b764060759222b2</a></p> 
                  <p>It is not possible to use Term Enum as a faceting method, for numeric and single valued fields ( org.apache.solr.request.SimpleFacets ) .</p> 
                  <p>We personally verified that there are use cases when this is bringing a quite big performance regression ( even with DocValues enabled) .<br> In the mailing list from time to time people complain about a regression happening with the term enum method, but actually it is more likely to be the automatic forcing of FCS.</p> 
                  <p>Forcing FCS in co-op with the famous regression that happened in <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/SOLR-8096" title="Major faceting performance regressions" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="SOLR-8096">SOLR-8096</a> could be confused as a term Enum regression.</p> 
                 </div> 
                </div>
