                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>AttributeSource/TokenStream inspection in Solr needs to have some insight into the contents of AttributeImpls. As <a href="https://issues.apache.org/jira/browse/LUCENE-2302" title="Replacement for TermAttribute+Impl with extended capabilities (byte[] support, CharSequence, Appendable)" class="issue-link" data-issue-key="LUCENE-2302"><del>LUCENE-2302</del></a> has some problems with toString() <span class="error">[which is not structured and conflicts with CharSequence's definition for CharTermAttribute]</span>, I propose an simple API that get a default implementation in AttributeImpl (just like toString() current):</p> 
                  <ul class="alternate" type="square"> 
                   <li>Iterator&lt;Map.Entry&lt;String,?&gt;&gt; AttributeImpl.contentsIterator() returns an iterator (for most attributes its a singleton) of a key-value pair, e.g. "term"<del>&gt;"foobar","startOffset"</del>&gt;Integer.valueOf(0),...</li> 
                   <li>AttributeSource gets the same method, it just concat the iterators of each getAttributeImplsIterator() AttributeImpl</li> 
                  </ul> 
                  <p>No backwards problems occur, as the default toString() method will work like before (it just gets iterator and lists), but we simply remove the documentation for the format. (Char)TermAttribute gets a special impl fo toString() according to CharSequence and a corresponding iterator.</p> 
                  <p>I also want to remove the abstract hashCode() and equals() methods from AttributeImpl, as they are not needed and just create work for the implementor.</p> 
                 </div>
