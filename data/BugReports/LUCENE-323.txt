                <h2 class="toggle-title">Description</h2>
               </div>
               <div class="mod-content">
                <div id="description-val" class="field-ignore-highlight"> 
                 <div class="user-content-block"> 
                  <p>The attached test case demonstrates this problem and provides a fix:<br> 1. Use a custom similarity to eliminate all tf and idf effects, just to <br> isolate what is being tested.<br> 2. Create two documents doc1 and doc2, each with two fields title and <br> description. doc1 has "elephant" in title and "elephant" in description. <br> doc2 has "elephant" in title and "albino" in description.<br> 3. Express query for "albino elephant" against both fields.<br> Problems:<br> a. MultiFieldQueryParser won't recognize either document as containing <br> both terms, due to the way it expands the query across fields.<br> b. Expressing query as "title:albino description:albino title:elephant <br> description:elephant" will score both documents equivalently, since each <br> matches two query terms.<br> 4. Comparison to MaxDisjunctionQuery and my method for expanding queries <br> across fields. Using notation that () represents a BooleanQuery and ( | ) <br> represents a MaxDisjunctionQuery, "albino elephant" expands to:<br> ( (title:albino | description:albino)<br> (title:elephant | description:elephant) )<br> This will recognize that doc2 has both terms matched while doc1 only has 1 <br> term matched, score doc2 over doc1.</p> 
                  <p>Refinement note: the actual expansion for "albino query" that I use is:<br> ( (title:albino | description:albino)~0.1<br> (title:elephant | description:elephant)~0.1 )<br> This causes the score of each MaxDisjunctionQuery to be the score of highest <br> scoring MDQ subclause plus 0.1 times the sum of the scores of the other MDQ <br> subclauses. Thus, doc1 gets some credit for also having "elephant" in the <br> description but only 1/10 as much as doc2 gets for covering another query term <br> in its description. If doc3 has "elephant" in title and both "albino" <br> and "elephant" in the description, then with the actual refined expansion, it <br> gets the highest score of all (whereas with pure max, without the 0.1, it <br> would get the same score as doc2).</p> 
                  <p>In real apps, tf's and idf's also come into play of course, but can affect <br> these either way (i.e., mitigate this fundamental problem or exacerbate it).</p> 
                 </div> 
                </div>
